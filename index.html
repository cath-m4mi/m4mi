a1

When it comes to putting music to a poem, I find it very difficult as how i interoperate them can change on a daily basis, and as an ongoing project my ideas kept changing. I went with the 3rd poem extract, which to me screamed out space. I created two sections rather than maintaining one, as I feel like completely dedicating the track to a trance genre could be boring, especially without any visuals to add to it to create a completely immersive sound. with a slight edge of unease, much how like being in space would be! I’d like to think that the silence and the significant increase in volume of the second section symbolises a potential run in with danger, and the abrupt ending leads to the listener being able to make up their own mind.


a2

This composition was incredibly fun to create, and really let me explore the use of my main instrument - percussion. I felt that a mix of tuned percussion (vibraphone, marimba and glockenspiel) fitted perfectly to create a magical vibe that the lights really portray. Personally, I found the lights reminded me of the idea of fairies, that are there but do not particularly want to be seen. I decided that the movement of light could portray either volume or pitch, so I decided that I would wait and see how the composition unfolded and as I came to significant light changes, I decided what would be the best path to follow in terms of whether I would change pitch or volume so the changes can come across subtle as I felt that this fitted the flow of the music better.


a3

Trying to connect the video with events in the track was incredibly difficult for this whist using Sibelius. Part of my inspiration for this piece of music was a piece of music called ‘Foundry’ by John Mackey. It is a wind band piece based on a steel work, which was a similar vibe that I got from this video. I went with a lot of brass, and a lot going on to mimic the constant movement and change in the video. In the second section, I used cymbals to mimic explosions as a lot of the news clips were symbolising war and chaos, and a lot of notes that didn’t seem to fit to create a feel of discomfort.


a3

As an avid gamer, this track was really fun to create. I created a piece of music that had a medieval classical feel, but something not completely overwhelming to the game as the main focus of it should be aiding concentration. This meant a lot of repetition and nothing that might distract the player from what they were doing. I ran a poll that I sent to friends in the gaming community on what they would want from a game such as this, and listened to soundtracks from similar games such as Hearthstone to see how the music correlated with the gameplay. All were much as I expected. Something that fitted the era of the theme of the game (in this case medieval fantasy) without being over the top.
